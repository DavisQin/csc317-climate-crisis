Effects of Global Warming

Global warming is one of the biggest environmental problems today. It affects people, animals, weather patterns, and the future of the planet.

Effect on Humans

Global warming increases heat waves, droughts, floods, and strong storms. These conditions can affect people's health and daily life.

Hot temperatures also impact farming, water supply, and living conditions in many regions around the world.

Effect on Natural Habitat

Animals and plants depend on stable environments. When temperatures change, many habitats start to disappear.

Melting ice, forest fires, and ocean warming affect wildlife and biodiversity. Some species must move to survive.

Predictions for the Future

Scientists believe the Earth will continue warming if greenhouse gases keep increasing.

Future effects could include rising sea levels, stronger storms, and more extreme climate events.

6 Quick Facts

  • Earth’s temperature has increased in the last century.
  • Carbon dioxide is a major greenhouse gas.
  • Glaciers are melting faster than before.
  • Sea levels are rising worldwide.
  • Some animals are losing their habitats.
  • Human action can reduce future warming.
